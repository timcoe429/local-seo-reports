02 — Technical SEO Foundation

Domain Authority & Backlink Profile

What This Means
Domain Authority (DA) is a predictive metric developed by Moz that indicates how likely your website is to rank in search engine results. Scores range from 1-100, with higher scores corresponding to greater ranking ability. Your DA of 11 provides a baseline competitive position but indicates significant room for improvement.
Why It Matters
DA directly impacts organic search visibility. Websites with higher DA scores rank above lower-scoring competitors for the same keywords. This translates to more website traffic, more leads, and more revenue. Your competitors with DA scores above 15-20 are capturing traffic that could be yours.
How To Improve
  • Build high-quality backlinks from authoritative industry sites
  • Get listed in local business directories
  • Create linkable content assets
  • Partner with complementary businesses for mutual linking
Key Insight
With 856 backlinks from 124 domains, your link acquisition velocity is strong. However, Trust Flow matching Domain Authority at 11 suggests these are primarily lower-authority links. Focus should shift to acquiring fewer, higher-quality links from authoritative sources (DA 40+) such as local news outlets, chamber of commerce, or industry associations.

07 — Conversion Optimization

Website CRO Analysis

Current State
Your website is professionally designed with clear service information. However, the homepage has competing CTAs (Schedule Now, Instant Quote, Book Now, Contact Us) and significant visual clutter. Primary CTA is "Contact Us" rather than direct booking. The 5.0 Google rating isn't prominently featured as a trust builder above the fold.
Problems Identified
  • Multiple competing CTAs create decision paralysis
  • "Contact Us" adds friction vs. "Book Now"
  • Review social proof buried below fold
  • Homepage complexity reduces conversion focus
  • No clear value proposition hierarchy
Recommendations
  • Single primary CTA: "Book Now" (not "Contact")
  • Feature "178 Five-Star Reviews" prominently
  • A/B test simplified landing page variant
  • Test CTA button colors and copy
  • Reduce visual elements competing for attention
Conversion Psychology
"Contact Us" implies additional steps and delays. Studies show direct action CTAs ("Book Now", "Schedule Now") convert 30-50% better than inquiry-based CTAs. Users who click "Contact Us" must then navigate forms, wait for response, and coordinate timing. "Book Now" implies immediate access and friction-free process. This single change could increase conversion rate from estimated 2-3% to 4-5%, doubling lead generation from existing traffic.

A/B Testing Priorities

1
Primary CTA Copy & Color
Test "Book Now" vs. "Contact Us" with blue (#0f58a8) vs. orange/red high-contrast buttons. Hypothesis: Direct booking language with contrasting color increases clicks by 40%+.
2
Social Proof Placement
Test homepage variant featuring "178 Five-Star Google Reviews" with star visuals above the fold vs. current placement. Add specific review quotes. Hypothesis: Prominent social proof increases trust and conversion by 25%+.
3
Simplified Landing Page
Create variant with 50% less content, single CTA, clearer visual hierarchy, and direct booking path. Remove competing CTAs and reduce decision points. Hypothesis: Simplified design increases conversion rate by 30-60%.
